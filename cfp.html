Proposal guidelines

How to write a strong proposal

📝

Format

Talks are 30 minutes including Q&A. State your topic, target audience, and key takeaways clearly. Each accepted talk includes one free speaker ticket for the full event.

🎯

Structure

Provide a short summary for the programme and a detailed description for reviewers. Spell out what attendees will learn and what prior knowledge is expected.

🔍

Review process

All proposals are reviewed double-blind. Reviewers assess relevance, clarity, technical depth, originality, and potential audience interest.

Common pitfalls

  • Sales pitches — reference your product if needed, but the audience values open, reproducible approaches. Sales pitches are typically rejected.
  • Repeated talks — we have a strong preference for new talks and new speakers. If your talk is already available online, it is unlikely to be accepted.
  • Future work — the core content should already be shaped. Don't rely on data or prototypes that don't yet exist.
  • Overly long proposals — good proposals typically cover everything in under 200 words.

Tips

  • Know your audience — state the expected background and experience level clearly.
  • Include a time breakdown — e.g. 0–10 min: intro, 10–20 min: demo, 20–30 min: Q&A.
  • Get feedback — ask colleagues to review your abstract before submitting.
  • First-time speaker? — mention it in your proposal. We'll reach out with mentorship opportunities.
