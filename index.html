◈ Departure date ◈ November 25–26, 2026 Centre International de Conférences de Sorbonne Université · Paris

A new annual conference for everyone who uses computation to make sense of the world — and believes that understanding your tools and algorithms is what gives you agency in a digital world.

Submit a Proposal 🛸 Learn more

Answers are easier to come by than ever. Understanding, trusting, and mastering the tools producing them? That's the hard part. And the interesting one.

From the organizers of PyData Paris, Jupyter, Apache Arrow, Mamba, Scikit-learn, and many more building our shared, powerful, accessible, and interpretable compute infrastructure.

Mission briefing

Built for the people who build with data

Where a biologist, a city planner, and a software engineer find themselves solving the same problem from different angles.

🔬

Talks & Keynotes

Deep dives into data analysis, visualization, reproducible science, and domain-specific tools across academia, industry, and public institutions.

🛠

Workshops

Hands-on sessions taught by the people who build the tools — going deep into technical skills, new features, and best practices.

🌐

Community Tracks

Dedicated side rooms for open source projects to meet, grow, and find their people. The ecosystem, in one room.

⚗️

Sprints

Collaborative coding sessions where contributors and newcomers work together on the tools that power modern data science.

Crew manifest

One third academia. One third industry. One third institutions.

Students and open source maintainers alongside established professionals and the decision-makers who shape how organizations use data. We mix the domains on purpose.

🎓

Academia

Researchers, educators, PhD students, and scientific computing practitioners pushing the frontier of reproducible science.

⚡

Digital Industry

Engineers, data scientists, open source maintainers, and the technical leaders who build and depend on the compute stack.

🏛

Public Institutions

Civil servants, policy analysts, and public sector teams using data to make decisions that affect everyone.
